## The Product

**Al-Khwarizmi's Notebook** — an Islamic A-Level mathematics curriculum.
Arc 0 ("Genesis") is the pre-FP1 foundation. Everything a student needs
before Further Pure Mathematics 1.

**The reader:** A 16-year-old Muslim student. Strong or weak in maths.
Not weak in faith, but has never seen faith and education as the same thing.

**The thesis:** Mathematics is READ from creation, not imposed on it. The
Quran generates mathematical problems through its commands. To obey — to
divide justly, to record debts, to observe the sky — you must compute.
The computation IS the worship made precise.

## The Core Principle

> **TENSION → NEED → TOOL → RELIEF**

The reader must WANT the formula before they see it. The problem comes
from a PERSON whose need the reader inhabits. The aha moment belongs
to the READER, not the narrator.

This is how the Quran teaches: "Allah instructs you CONCERNING YOUR
CHILDREN" (4:11) — it places you in the situation, THEN gives the rule.

**If the tool arrives before the tension, the island has failed.**
**If the person vanishes and only math remains, the island has failed.**

The document writes the visuals. "Who taught by the pen." (96:4)

## The Voice

First person. "I" — Al-Khwarizmi discovering. Timeless setting.

### What he sounds like

A person THINKING, not explaining. Terse in discovery. Shows through
ACTION, not narration.

> "A man has died."

> "I stop. 27/24. That is greater than 1."

> "The wife is watching me. She has seen the number I wrote."

### Two voice tests (every paragraph)

**Test A — Would a real person say this?** If no, cut.
**Test B — Is the narrator showing or telling?** If telling, rewrite.

1. **No LaTeX.** Code blocks for display math, backticks for inline.

4. **Mark allocations** on Hasan and Sahih problems.
