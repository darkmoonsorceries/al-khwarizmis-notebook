# Prologue — The Impossible Sum

بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم

A man has died.

His wife washes the courtyard stones because her hands need work. Two daughters sit against the far wall, the younger one's head on her sister's shoulder. The father stands in the doorway — he has buried his son and has not yet spoken. The mother arrived before anyone sent word. She is already seated. She is waiting.

They have come for what the Quran commands: just division of what the dead man left behind.

The shares are exact. Not counsel. Not suggestion. Fractions cut by revelation:

| Heir | Share | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| Wife | 1/8 | 4:12 |
| Two daughters | 2/3 | 4:11 |
| Father | 1/6 | 4:11 |
| Mother | 1/6 | 4:11 |

I take a common denominator. Twenty-four.

<!-- diagram: heir-shares -->

> *Three heirs fill the estate. Twenty-three parts out of twenty-four. It fits. Barely.*

I add the mother's share. Four more twenty-fourths.

```
3 + 16 + 4 + 4 = 27
```

I stop.

<!-- diagram: overflow -->

> *Twenty-seven twenty-fourths. The shares demand more than the estate contains.*

27/24. That is greater than 1.

Each fraction is correct — I have checked each against the Book. Together they exceed what exists. The mathematics does not close. There is no rounding error. There is no misreading. Four commands from Allah, and they sum to more than the whole.

The wife is watching me. She does not read mathematics, but she reads faces, and she has read mine.

I cannot solve this. I do not have a method for fractions that exceed their whole. I do not have equations. I do not have a way to restore what is broken while preserving what each heir is owed. I reach further back and find I do not even have a clear definition of what "equal" means. The word I have been writing — this "=" — I cannot say what makes it true.

Every tool I need is missing. Not one. All of them.

I set the paper on the table. I look at the family. The younger daughter has not moved from her sister's shoulder. The father has still not spoken. The mother is still waiting.

I know one thing that I did not learn and cannot doubt:

<!-- diagram: ground -->

> *A thing is itself. From this I will build what they need.*

A thing is itself. From this I will build what they need. Fractions. Equations. A method to restore the impossible sum to balance. Every tool, from the ground, from the only fact that needs no proof.

The family is waiting. The mathematics is not ready. I will make it ready.

*1 = 1 has not been lost.*
